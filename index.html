Research

I am currently conducting two independent research projects under Dr. Jessica Cusick and Dr. Craig Thulin. Below is a brief overview of both projects in more detail.
Maternal effects in Phodopus sungorus

This project under Dr. Jess Cusick investigates the effects of maternal stress and maternal microbiome manipulations on offspring social behaviors. A summary of the results of this project can be found here (currently drafting manuscript for publication).

Development of the midwestern blot

In Dr. Thulin's lab, my work focuses on the development of a new protein blotting method we are calling the midwestern blot. Instead of using complex labeling techniques (e.g., Western, Far-Western), the midwestern uses a known ligand to screen for proteins of interest. Our lab is interested in novel derivatives of the pigment xanthommatin recently identified in monarch butterfly wings (Forman and Thulin, 2022). As xanthommatin is visible to the naked eye, the midwestern may allow us to isolate xanthommatin-binding proteins from monarch butterfly pupae, with the goal of elucidating novel enzymes responsible for the synthesis of the novel deaminated xanthommatin derivatives.
